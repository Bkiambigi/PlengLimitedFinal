New Ventures: Behind the Scenes

Explore the timeline below to see the progress and milestones of our projects.
Date: 15th May 2024

UON COMPLEX UNIVERSITY OF NAIROBI

The project has been conceived to house the functions of the Center for Land Acquisition and Resettlement Studies (CLEARS). CLEARS is dedicated to developing practice-oriented training programs in land acquisition and resettlement. In addition to training, CLEARS will conduct practice-oriented research in these fields. The center aims to provide comprehensive education and research opportunities to enhance the skills and knowledge of professionals involved in land acquisition and resettlement processes.
Date: 16th May 2024

SAFARICOM/MPESA PROJECTS

Since December 2023, we have been collaborating with Safaricom and M-Pesa on a variety of renovation and design projects for schools. Some of the major projects include Chuka Township Primary School, Starehe Girls' Centre, and Kamuiru Primary School, among others. These efforts are part of our commitment to enhancing educational facilities and supporting the community. By improving the infrastructure of these schools, we aim to create better learning environments for students, thereby contributing to the overall development and empowerment of the community. Our work involves modernizing classrooms, upgrading facilities, and incorporating innovative design solutions to ensure a positive and lasting impact.
Date: 17th May 2024 AFFORDABLE HOUSING (MATHIOYA TVC)
The project aims to design and construct a hostel facility with a capacity to accommodate 540 students in Mathioya. This initiative is intended to provide safe, comfortable, and conducive living environments for students, thereby enhancing their overall educational experience. The design will incorporate modern engineering principles and sustainable practices to ensure the hostel meets high standards of safety, efficiency, and environmental responsibility. The construction will involve the latest building technologies and materials to create a durable and cost-effective solution, addressing the growing need for student housing in the region.
Date: 23rd Feb 2024

STANBIC HEAD OFFICE CAMPUS WESTLAND

The project includes the construction of a proposed reception pavilion and the renovation of the existing floor office. The renovations will involve re-organizing the space to improve functionality and aesthetics. Additionally, a mezzanine structure will be added to maximize the use of vertical space and provide additional working or storage areas. The overall aim is to create a more efficient, welcoming, and modern environment for both employees and visitors.
